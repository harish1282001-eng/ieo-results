Indian Engineering Olympiad 2018: NIT Trichy, JNTUK, and Jadavpur University Students Clinch Top Honors

Abhishek Raj secures AIR 1 from National Institute of Technology Trichy, followed by Aguru Manoj Kumar from JNTUK and Suresh Varma from Jadavpur University in prestigious national-level engineering competition
Results declared April 11, 2018 | Report published April 12, 2018

In a remarkable display of academic excellence, three students from premier technical institutions have emerged as national toppers in the Indian Engineering Olympiad (IEO) 2018, showcasing the extraordinary talent pool present across India's diverse engineering landscape. The results, declared on April 11, 2018, by the Gate Forum in collaboration with McGraw Hill Education, have brought laurels to National Institute of Technology (NIT) Trichy, Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University Kakinada (JNTUK), and Jadavpur University Kolkata.

Abhishek Raj, a third-year Civil Engineering student at National Institute of Technology, Trichy, has secured the coveted All India Rank (AIR) 1, demonstrating exceptional proficiency in core engineering concepts and problem-solving abilities. His achievement has not only brought pride to his institution but has also established NIT Trichy as a center of engineering excellence on the national map.

Following closely, Aguru Manoj Kumar from JNTUK University has claimed AIR 2. Also pursuing third-year Civil Engineering from the Visakhapatnam center, Manoj's achievement demonstrates the rising standards of technical education in Andhra Pradesh and the capability of regional universities to compete at the highest national level.

Completing the podium, Suresh Varma from Jadavpur University, Kolkata, has secured AIR 3 in the same category. A third-year Civil Engineering student, Suresh's success underscores the continued academic rigor and quality of education at one of India's oldest and most respected universities. His performance from the Kolkata examination center highlights the strong engineering tradition present in Eastern India.

Examination Details

The 3rd Indian Engineering Olympiad (IEO 2018) was organized by Thinkcell Learning Solutions Pvt Ltd and conducted on 25th February 2018 across multiple examination centers in India. The pan-India exam offers a real test of engineering aptitude for engineering students in their respective fields on a national platform.

Over 2,82,000 students from more than 500 engineering colleges across the country registered for this edition of the exam, with nearly 1,52,000 students registering only from the 3rd year of civil engineering, making it one of the largest competitive examinations for engineering students in India.

Institutional Reactions

National Institute of Technology, Trichy has expressed immense pride in Abhishek Raj's achievement. The Director, in an official statement issued on April 12, 2018, congratulated the student and credited the institution's focus on research-oriented learning and industry collaboration for this success.

JNTUK University has celebrated Aguru Manoj Kumar's success as a testament to the quality of technical education in Andhra Pradesh. The Vice-Chancellor announced special recognition for the student and promised continued support for future endeavors.

Jadavpur University officials highlighted Suresh Varma's consistent academic performance and dedication as key factors behind his AIR 3 ranking. The university, known for its strong engineering legacy, sees this as validation of its rigorous academic standards.

The toppers of Indian Engineering Olympiad 2018 are now positioned for exceptional career opportunities.
